HOW DOES AN INSURANCE WORKS?
Insurance is a way to protect yourself from financial loss in the event of unexpected incidents, such as accidents, illnesses, or damage to your property. When you purchase an insurance policy, you agree to pay a regular fee called a premium. In return, the insurance company promises to cover certain costs if something happens that is covered by your policy.
BENEFITS OF AN INSURANCE
  • Financial protection against unexpected events.
  • Offers reassurance by reducing worry about unexpected costs.
  • Ensures you meet legal requirements and avoid penalties.
  • Safeguards valuable assets like your home and car.
  • Provides assistance during emergencies to help you recover.
FAQs

How does insurance work?

You pay regular premiums to an insurance company. If a covered event occurs, the company helps cover the associated costs, either by paying directly or reimbursing you.

What type of insurance do i need?

The types of insurance you need depend on your personal and financial situation, but common ones include health, life, auto, home, and liability insurance.

Difference between term life and whole life insurance?

Term life insurance provides coverage for a specific period, while whole life insurance covers you for your entire life and may include an investment component.

What is a premium?

A premium is the amount you pay, usually monthly or annually, to keep your insurance policy active.

What is an insurance?

Insurance is a contract where you pay a premium in exchange for financial protection against specific risks, such as accidents, illnesses, or property damage.
